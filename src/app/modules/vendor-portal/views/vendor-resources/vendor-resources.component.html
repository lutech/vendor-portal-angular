Vendor Resources

Follow our step by step guide below to find out how to start selling to the Federal Government.

1: Getting Ready

Take the training needed to get started.

  • Training

    You must complete the "Pathways to Success" training and the "Readiness Assessment", which are both free and will help you understand the GSA MAS Program.

  • Federal Acquistion Regulation (FAR)

    Purchases made by the federal government must comply with the Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR), which governs all federal acquisitions. Learn about the variety of different ways that agencies can comply with these regulations.

  • SBA Learning

    Educate yourself on entrepreneurial best practices and available financing options with the SBA's digital learning platform.

2: Register

Register your company in several places before you submit your offer.

  • System for Award Management (SAM)

    You must have an active registration in SAM to do business with the federal government.

  • Unique Entity Identifier

    GSA uses the Unique Entity Identifier (UEI) to identify your business, and the UEI is required to do business with us. The Unique Entity ID (SAM) is generated at SAM.gov

3: How to Get a Contract

elow is a summary of the necessary steps to obtain and maintain a MAS Information Technology contract

  • Mulitple Award Schedule (MAS)

    Although the Schedule is the premier vehicle for government sales, including federal, state, and local government agencies, they are not the only option. Learn more about the Multiple Award Schedule (MAS) or Schedule contract.

  • FASt Lane Eligibility

    With FASt Lane, eligible vendors get shorter processing times for contract actions that directly support federal customer agency requirements, and agencies get fast access to emerging technologies and innovative offerings.

  • Startup Springboard

    We want to get the latest technologies to federal agencies faster. Startup Springboard gets innovative IT companies with fewer than two years of experience onto the Information Technology Category.

4: How to Market to the Government

To succeed with your MAS Information Technology contract, you'll need to actively promote your business to potential government customers.

  • The Solicitation Package

    The solicitation package provides guidance to industry partners who wish to offer information technology (IT) products, services, and solutions to federal, state, and local government customers on the MAS Information Technology contract vehicle.

  • FedBizOpps

    FedBizOpps provides a comprehensive database of all major government solicitations, contract awards, subcontracting opportunities, surplus property sales, and foreign business opportunities with the federal government.

  • Opportunities for Small Business

    Our procurement programs offer great ways for small businesses to partner with the agency. The Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization (OSDBU) helps provide access to these opportunities by pointing out the steps to success.

5: How to Prepare an Offer

Learn how to complete the required forms and familarize yourself with the most recent offer templates.

  • Available Offerings

    The Multiple Award Schedule (MAS or the Schedule) solicitation is broken into Large Categories, Subcategories, and Special Item Numbers (SIN) that have corresponding North American Industry Classification System (NAICS). Find out how to identify the large categories, subcategories, and SINs currently available under MAS.

  • Requirements and Attachments

    Depending on the products or services your company would like to offer on Schedule, additional certifications, requirements or attachments may be required as part of your offer submission.

  • Assembling the Offer

    Assembling your offer for a Schedule contract is a multi-step process that will require work and time. You'll need to gather and fill out documents about your company's finances, experience, products and services offered, and your company's business dealings.

  • Offers/Mods

    Congratulations! You have completed the guide. Now, let's get started on your first offer.
